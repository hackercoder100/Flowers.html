Hydrangea (Hydrangea)

About the Hydrangea

Hydrangea is a genus of over 75 species of flowering plants native to Asia and the Americas. It is a popular ornamental shrub, widely cultivated for its large, showy flower heads that bloom from summer to fall.

Characteristics

Hydrangea flowers are produced in large clusters (corymbs), typically in "mophead" or "lacecap" forms. For many species, the flower color is affected by the soil's pH level: acidic soil produces blue flowers, while alkaline soil results in pink or red flowers. The colorful "petals" are actually modified leaves called sepals.
